5 years in Mangalore introduced me to such a different array of cuisine which I had never tried before. Mangalorean cuisine mainly comprises of dishes from the Tulu Nadu region (Udupi-Mangalore-Kasargod) and is quite unique. Today I am sharing the recipe of one of the famous Mangalorean dishes (and one of my favourites), chicken ghee roast with neer dosa. Chicken ghee roast, being spicy and little heavy, goes perfectly with light and spongy neer dosa. (A match made in heaven.) So let’s see how to cook this delicious combo. NEER DOSA INGREDIENTS
  • 1 cup of rice
  • 1/4th cup coconut
  • 5 cups of water
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil
PROCEDURE
  • Soak the rice in 1 cup of water for 2 hours.
  • After 2 hours, blend the soaked rice with coconut into a smooth paste. (Peel the brown skin off the coconut before using.)
  • Add about 4 cups of water into the paste to make it into pouring consistency.
  • Add salt to taste.
  • Take a non-stick dosa tawa (if not, use regular non-stick tawa) and put it on medium-high flame.
  • Take some paper napkins, dip them into little bit of oil and grease the tawa nicely.
  • When the tawa becomes hot enough (You can check it by splashing a little bit of water on the tawa. If it splatters then the tawa is hot enough.), pour in a ladle of the dosa batter onto the tawa (Do mix the batter nicely every time before pouring it onto the tawa) and spread the batter nicely over the entire circumference. The dosa need to be porous, having little holes in it.
  • Cook until the sides of the dosa start separating from the tawa and the centre isn’t sticky. You need not cook it on the other side.
  • Fold the dosa into quarters and carefully remove it onto a plate. Your neer dosa is ready.
CHICKEN GHEE ROAST INGREDIENTS
  • 400 g chicken
  • ½ cup of curd
  • ½ tsp of turmeric
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp coriander seeds
  • 2 tsp cumin seeds
  • ½ tsp fennel seeds
  • 1½ tsp black peppercorns
  • 4 cloves
  • 10 dry kashmiri chillies
  • 5-7 dry red chillies
  • 1½ inch ginger
  • 4-5 big cloves of garlic
  • 2 tsp tamarind pulp soaked in water for 30 minutes)
  • 1 onion chopped
  • 1½ tsp jaggery
  • 6-7 cashews
  • 6-7 tsp ghee
  • Salt to taste
  • Water
PROCEDURE
  • Marinate the chicken in curd, turmeric and lemon juice for 1 hour.
  • In a pan, dry roast kashmiri chillies, dry red chillies, coriander seeds, cumin seeds, fennel seeds, black peppercorns and cloves. Roast them till they brown a little and are fragrant. Do not burn the spices.
  • Let the spices cool. Blend the spices with ginger, garlic, tamarind pulp and some water till you get a smooth red paste.
  • After an hour, add salt to taste to the marinated chicken.
  • Add 5-6 tsp ghee into a pan. (You can add even more if you want.)
  • When the ghee is hot enough, add onions and sauté till light golden brown.
  • Add the marinated chicken pieces and fry them for 3-4 minutes on medium-high heat.
  • Add the spice paste to the chicken and mix everything well till all the chicken is nicely coated. Cook everything for another 3-4 minutes.
  • Add salt to taste.
  • Add jaggery to it. Then add 1 cup of water and cook it covered on medium-low flame till the chicken get properly cooked.
  • While the chicken is cooking; in another pan, add 1 tsp of ghee and roast the cashews nicely till golden brown. Add the roasted cashews to the chicken.
  • When the chicken is almost cooked, take the lid off and cook it till you get a semi-dry consistency. Your chicken ghee roast is ready.
Hope you give these recipes a try and enjoy the chicken ghee roast with neer dosa.
