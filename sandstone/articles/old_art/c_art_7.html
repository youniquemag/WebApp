Summer’s here. And in India, most of the households will be busy with pickling raw mangoes with a whole mix of different spices. I love these tangy and aromatic pickles. If you are also a lover of pickles, I am sure you would love this recipe of Achari Chicken too. A famous North Indian dish, it consists of cooking chicken in tangy gravy with pickling spices. So let’s take a look at the recipe. INGREDIENTS
  • 500 gm of chicken (preferably cut into small pieces)
  • ¼ tsp onion seeds
  • 1 tsp black mustard seeds
  • 1/8 tsp fenugreek seeds
  • ¾ tsp fennel seeds
  • ¾ tsp coriander seeds crushed
  • 2 medium sized onions chopped
  • 2-3 green chillies chopped
  • 1½ tsp garlic paste
  • ¾ tsp ginger paste
  • 2 medium sized tomatoes chopped
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • ½ tsp Kashmiri chilli powder
  • ½ tsp red chilli powder (or according to your taste)
  • ½ cup hung curd
  • ½ inch ginger julienned
  • 3 tbsp mustard oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Pinch of sugar
  • Water
Optional:
  • Few coriander leaves chopped for garnishing
  • 3-4 green chillies
  • 1 tsp chaat masala
PROCEDURE
  • Add the mustard oil to a hot pan. (If you don’t like mustard oil, you may use any other vegetable oil; though mustard oil will give you the best results.)
  • Once the oil starts to smoke up, add the onion seeds, mustard seeds, fenugreek seeds, fennel seeds and crushed coriander seeds. Let the spices splutter.
  • Add chopped onions to it and sauté till the onions are translucent. Add ginger and garlic paste and cook for 1-2 minutes on a medium to medium-high flame.
  • Add the chopped tomatoes to it and cook till the tomatoes become soft.
  • Add the chicken pieces. Cook everything until the chicken pieces aren’t pink.
  • Add turmeric powder, red chilli powder and Kashmiri red chilli powder. Stir for a minute.
  • Lower the flame and add the hung curd. (Mix about 1 tsp of gram flour to well beaten hung curd to prevent the curd from splitting.) Mix everything well.
  • Add salt and sugar. Cook it covered over a medium flame for 5 minutes.
  • Add julienned ginger pieces and cook it covered for another 5-7 minutes or until the chicken gets cooked thoroughly. (If you feel that the gravy is drying up, add little warm water to it while cooking.) Your Achari Chicken is ready.
  • For garnishing:- Slit some green chillies half the way. Mix the chaat masala with little water to form a paste. Stuff the slit green chillies with this chaat masala paste and fry them in a small pan. Garnish the Achari Chicken with chopped coriander leaves and fried tangy green chillies.
